Conference Registration Now Open!
All of us at oSTEM Incorporated are proud to announce that oSTEM’s 4th National Annual Conference is a joint conference, hosted with the National Organization of Gay and Lesbian Scientists and Technical Professionals (NOGLSTP), and their Out to Innovate 2014 Summit.

Registration is now open for our 2014 joint conference, being held in Atlanta, Georgia on November 7-9. Register soon! Conference registrations for oSTEM students will increase to $50 after October 1.

There will be 30 breakout sessions with 6 concurrent workshops at a time! This year’s summit will include presentations from many notable champions of and examples of LGBT diversity in STEM including:
  • Tam O’Shaugnessy, Co-founder and CEO of Sally Ride Science
  • Lynn Conway, pioneering electrical engineer
  • Kei Koizumi, Assistant Director, Federal Research and Development, White House Office of Science and Technology Policy
  • LaDoris “Dot” Harris, Director of the Office of Economic Impact and Diversity at the United States Department of Energy
  • Donna Riley, Program Director at National Science Foundation and Professor of Engineering Education at Virginia Tech
  • Jack Andraka, Recipient of the 2012 Gordon E. Moore Award at the Intel International Science and Engineering Fair
  • Archie Ervin, Vice President of Institute Diversity for Georgia Institute of Technology
Attending sponsors for oSTEM include Danaher, Genentech, GoDaddy, Google, GM, Alcoa, Raytheon, Lockheed Martin, Central Intelligence Agency, BASF, Capitol One, and Goldman Sachs. Other sponsors include Battelle Memorial Institute, The Clorox Company, Coca-Cola, Johnson & Johnson, and Savannah River Nuclear Solutions.

Facebook Group for the oSTEM Trans Working Group
Based on discussions from the 2013 National Conference, oSTEM now has a Facebook group dedicated to its trans members! It will allow you to better connect with one another, and with the TWG leadership.

Although we greatly value the work done by our cis allies, we ask that only trans and non-binary identified people join this group. As we are just starting up, it will be a Closed group, meaning that if you are a member of the group your name will be visible, but your posts will not. This will make it easier to find as we build the group. After this year’s National Conference we’ll have a discussion on whether a Closed group continues to meet your needs, or if we should change it to a Secret group where no one can see your name on the group.

We’re looking forward to getting to know you better!
